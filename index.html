Aims And Objectives.

The Laboratory of Process Engineering, Computer Science and Mathematics (LIPIM) organizes the third edition of the Doctoral days of Mathematics and Engineering Sciences event ("Journées doctorale en Mathématiques et Science d'Ingénieur - JDMSI'2023").

JDMSI'2023 is an opportunity for PhD students to present their innovative research work and discuss it with a community of specialists in the field.

This event brings together professors, scientists, researchers and professionals from the academic community to explore the latest advances in mathematics and engineering science.

Doctoral students will have the opportunity to present their research in detail to an attentive audience, to obtain constructive feedback and to meet potential colleagues and mentors.

These days provide an opportunity to discover new ideas and approaches in the fields of mathematics and engineering science, which can stimulate future collaboration and cooperation.

In short, it is a stimulating platform for doctoral students to share their passion for research and enrich their academic career.

Call For Abstracts.

Prospective authors are kindly encouraged to contribute to and help shape the doctoral days through submissions of their research abstracts, extended abstracts. Submissions should contain original results obtained by the participant which demonstrates his recent advances in any topic related to the fields of interest.

The abstracts must be in English and the number of pages should not exceed 2 pages. We urge our participants to include a concise and clear description of their works in the form of a clear title, an abstract motivating the topic and describing the results as well as references and keywords.

All abstracts will be reviewed by the committee based on their originality, significance, methodological soundness, and clarity of exposition.

Deadlines

Submissions of Abstracts should be made before April 30, 2023 to be considered for communication in this event.

  • Submission : April 30, 2023
  • Acceptance Notification : May 10, 2023
  • Final Program : May 15, 2023
  • Conference Days : May 22-23, 2023

Best Presentation Prize will be awarded to one winner having the most outstanding communication of the entire conference.


A selected judging committee made up of Fellows and members of the conference committee will watch all the communications and decide on the winner.

Below criteria are taken into consideration for this award and marks are given out of 100.

  • 1-Content of the presentation (45%)
  • 2-Clarity and flow of the Presentation (20%)
  • 3- Novelty /contribution to the field (25%)
  • 4-Timing (10%)
